ğŸª‘ "Khreshchatyk" is one of the busiest and most chaotic stations, with passengers either exiting to the city center, transferring to the blue line, or just starting their journey. At such a pace, it's hard to appreciate the interior, but there's plenty to see! Given that "Khreshchatyk" is the central station of the city, the design team drew inspiration from Ukrainian art (which is why you can see corn and sunflowers above the pylons on the platform). But the most interesting feature is the majolica panel by Kyiv artist Oksana Hruzynska. It's an imitation of a Ukrainian carpet with 16 individually designed tiles, where no pattern or composition repeats! But the main thing at this station is that you can actually find a seat ;)
